What is Mediation?

A voluntary, confidential process facilitated by a neutral mediator

Mediation is a structured, private process where an impartial mediator helps disputing parties communicate, explore options and reach a mutually acceptable settlement. It is non-adversarial, flexible and focuses on practical, durable resolutions instead of courtroom rulings.

  • Confidential & Voluntary
  • Parties control the outcome
  • Cost and time efficient
  • Preserves relationships where possible

Legal Framework

Key statutes and provisions that support mediation in India
The Mediation Act, 2023
  • Comprehensive legal framework for mediation
  • Pre-litigation and institutional mediation covered
  • Confidentiality & enforceability safeguards
Enacted September 2023
Section 89, CPC 1908
  • Courts may refer disputes to ADR
  • Encourages mediation, arbitration, conciliation
  • Reduces court backlog
Amended 2002
Commercial Courts Act, 2015
  • Mandatory pre-suit mediation for some commercial disputes
  • Expedited process for business disputes
Amended 2018, 2023
Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996
  • Settlements recorded as awards where applicable
  • Enforceable like court decrees
Amended 2015–2021
